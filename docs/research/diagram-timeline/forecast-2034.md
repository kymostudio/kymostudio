## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The canvas SDK is in everything

The bet tldraw's 2025 Series A named — the infinite canvas as an embeddable developer primitive — matures into ubiquity: project tools, incident dashboards, agent-design platforms, and learning products all ship a canvas, and most of them ship the same few SDKs. The canvas stops being a destination and becomes a component, which is why an SDK sits at #4 on a list of "tools."

### 2. Behavior maps become a governance requirement

What entered last year as a safety-review artifact hardens into procurement language: enterprises buying agentic systems ask for the behavior map the way they ask for a SOC 2 report. Visual governance artifacts get template libraries, audit trails, and a consulting industry.

### 3. Spatial review finds its niche: the room, not the desk

Spatial diagram review settles where it is actually better — multi-party design reviews, incident walk-throughs, onboarding — while day-to-day diagram work stays on flat screens. The pattern matches every peripheral wave on this timeline: the new medium takes the ceremonies, not the routine.
