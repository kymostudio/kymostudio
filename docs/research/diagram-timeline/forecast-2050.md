## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Fifty-six years in: the timeline takes stock

From Unified Method 0.8 at OOPSLA to ratified intent maps: the diagram's journey runs desktop file → standard notation → web document → shared canvas → structured data → derived view → binding agreement. Each era kept the previous ones alive underneath it; nothing on this list ever quite dies — it either becomes infrastructure or becomes a niche, and both are forms of immortality.

### 2. The intent map reaches the types top 3

The three tenses get a fourth member: what is (live view, #1), what was designed (flowchart, #2), what *should be allowed* (intent, #3), what could be (simulation, #4). That a normative artifact ranks above a predictive one says what Era F is about.

### 3. Provenance reaches the tools top 3

Symmetrically, the verification layer passes the spatial layer in tools. At mid-era, the pattern is unmistakable: the categories still growing are the ones about *trusting and governing* pictures, not producing them. Production was solved decades ago; legitimacy is the frontier.
