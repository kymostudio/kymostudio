## Highlights

### 1. FigJam launches; tldraw open-sourced

On **21 April 2021** Figma launched **FigJam** — a lightweight whiteboard for sticky notes, connectors, shapes and freehand drawing — bringing the Miro-style canvas inside the biggest design platform: validation that visual collaboration was core workflow. The same year **Steve Ruiz open-sourced tldraw**, a shape-agnostic infinite-canvas library with a clean document model — treating the canvas as a **reusable engineering component**, foreshadowing its "canvas as SDK" positioning.

### 2. Visio comes to Microsoft 365 for everyone

Announced 9 June 2021: a lightweight Visio web app added to all commercial Microsoft 365 subscriptions at no extra cost. The incumbent's defensive answer to Lucidchart/Miro/draw.io — and an admission that diagramming had become a baseline collaboration feature rather than a $580 desktop SKU.

### 3. Excalidraw+ launches

On 3 May 2021 the Excalidraw team turned the viral open-source whiteboard (100k+ monthly users at the time) into a hosted product with workspaces, persistence and team collaboration — the open-source-to-SaaS template later echoed by tldraw.

## Context

With Miro, FigJam, Excalidraw and tldraw all thriving by end-2021, differentiation shifted from *having* a canvas to *what the canvas can do* (structure, code, and soon AI). This repo's canvas-engine work — tldraw initially embedded, later replaced by kymo's own engine — engages directly with this lineage.
