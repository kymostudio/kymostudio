## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The diagram becomes the negotiation surface

Era F's defining shift: the most consequential diagrams are no longer records (Era A–C) or derivations (D–E) but *agreements* — shared representations that human teams and machine systems both commit to act under. When a human approves a view, that approval binds the agents who plan against it; the picture is the contract's most legible clause.

### 2. The open canvas commons enters its second quarter-century

Excalidraw turns 26; diagrams.net is 35. The open, self-hostable canvas layer has now outlived three proprietary generations (desktop, SaaS, agent-era platforms), holding the floor for the same reason SQLite and Linux hold theirs: neutrality is a feature no commercial layer can offer.

### 3. The liveview-first organization is the norm

The pattern that was avant-garde in 2039 — no hand-maintained architecture documents at all, only derived views plus human-authored intent — is now simply how organizations above a certain complexity operate. The hand-drawn architecture diagram completes its move into the museum wing: pedagogy, proposals, and history.
