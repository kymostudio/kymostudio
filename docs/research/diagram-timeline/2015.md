## Highlights

### 1. Figma's invite-only multiplayer preview

On **3 December 2015**, after ~3 years of stealth development, **Figma launched a free, invite-only preview** of its browser-based, real-time collaborative design tool — the first time genuine multiplayer editing of visual canvases reached real users at scale. This opens Era C: every later collaborative canvas (FigJam, Miro's growth, Excalidraw, tldraw) follows the multiplayer-in-the-browser pattern Figma proved here.

### 2. OMG publishes UML 2.5

Formally published in 2015 after ~4 years of simplification work, **UML 2.5** was a complete rewrite-for-readability of the spec — the last major refresh of the language that had defined software diagramming for two decades, landing just as developer culture drifted toward lighter, ad-hoc diagrams.

### 3. Visio 2016 ships — the desktop paradigm's high-water mark

Released 22 September 2015 with Office 2016 (one-step Excel data linking, starter diagrams, IRM). Historically notable as the high-water mark of the desktop-first, file-based diagramming paradigm — right before browser-native collaborative tools took over the narrative. (Also ~2015: Structurizr, Simon Brown's "C4 diagrams as code" tooling, became publicly usable — exact launch date unpinned.)

## Context

The engineering was Figma's moat: a custom C++ rendering pipeline and operational-transform-style sync, years before browsers made this easy. The public launch followed in September 2016.
