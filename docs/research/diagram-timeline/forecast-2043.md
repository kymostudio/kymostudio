## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Procurement asks for signed views

Enterprise buyers standardize the requirement: any platform that renders system views must attest their derivation chain. Provenance climbs to #6 in tools on contract language rather than enthusiasm — the SOC 2 adoption curve, replayed for diagrams.

### 2. The DAG exits, folded into live and simulation views

The data-pipeline/DAG diagram — on the list since the 2017 data-engineering wave — leaves as a standalone type. Pipelines did not disappear; their pictures stopped being a genre, because the live view renders them like any other subsystem and the emerging what-if tooling animates them. The exit previews next year's entrant.

### 3. View history becomes replayable

Live-architecture platforms add the archive dimension: scrub the system's architecture backward through time, watch a migration land, replay the topology during last spring's incident. The diagram gains what code got from version control two generations earlier — and incident review, compliance, and onboarding all reorganize around it.
