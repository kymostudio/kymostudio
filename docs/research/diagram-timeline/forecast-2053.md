## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Provenance & trust layers reach #2 in tools

Eleven years after entering, the verification layer outranks every production surface except the ambient assistants. The list has never before had a #2 that neither renders nor hosts a single diagram — it only answers whether the diagram should be believed. In an era where anything can be generated instantly, the scarce service is the believing.

### 2. Public-sector transparency adopts the era's artifacts

Government systems begin publishing their intent maps and behavior maps as civic documents — the visual layer of algorithmic-accountability law. The genre built for boardrooms and regulators finds a third audience: citizens. Adoption is uneven and the renderings are contested, which is precisely what happens when an artifact starts to matter.

### 3. Agent-design platforms fade toward the ambient layer

The category that defined Era D slides to #8: not displaced by a rival, but evaporating upward — its review primitives absorbed into the ambient assistants, its canvases into the SDK layer. The timeline's recurring exit ramp (absorption, not defeat) claims its own era-winner for the first time.
