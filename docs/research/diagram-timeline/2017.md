## Highlights

### 1. Whimsical founded — fast, browser-native diagramming for everyone

**Whimsical** (Kaspars Dancis, Steve Schoeffel, 2017) combined flowcharts, wireframes, mind maps and sticky notes on one fast infinite canvas. It typifies the late-2010s shift toward **simple diagramming for non-specialists**: minimal learning curve, opinionated styling, speed — flowcharts that look good by default. It broadened *who* makes diagrams: PMs and founders, not just engineers and architects.

### 2. GitLab ships native Mermaid rendering

**GitLab 10.3 (December 2017)** became the first major code platform to render Mermaid text blocks as diagrams in markdown, issues and wikis — the first mainstream "diagrams-as-code in your repo" integration, four years before GitHub followed.

### 3. draw.io ships its desktop app

JGraph released the official offline Electron build of draw.io (May 2017), taking the free web editor to Windows/macOS/Linux and to security-sensitive users who can't put diagrams in the cloud — the start of draw.io's "security-first, fully offline-capable" positioning.

## Context

2017 is a quieter year between Figma's launch (2016) and the platform moves (2018). Whimsical's lesson — **defaults are the product**; most users never restyle — would be vindicated by every successful tool that followed.
