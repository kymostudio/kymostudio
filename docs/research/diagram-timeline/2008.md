## Highlights

### 1. WebSequenceDiagrams — text-to-diagram as a web service

**WebSequenceDiagrams**, by Steve Hanov, had its first commit in September 2008: type a plain-text description of interactions, get a rendered UML sequence diagram instantly in the browser. One of the earliest popular "diagram-as-text" *web services* and a direct inspiration for later libraries — it distilled diagram-as-code to its purest form for one diagram type.

### 2. Balsamiq Mockups launches

Former Adobe engineer Peldi Guilizzoni launched his one-man company's deliberately sketchy, low-fidelity wireframing tool on 19 June 2008. Revenue hit ~$100K within 5 months and $6M/yr within six years with zero funding — the clearest market evidence of a pent-up wireframing demand wave that reshaped what "diagramming" meant for product teams.

### 3. Raphaël.js released

Dmitry Baranovskiy released **Raphaël**, a small JS library abstracting SVG and IE's VML behind one API — making cross-browser vector graphics practical years before IE supported SVG. It became the rendering layer for countless browser diagram tools (js-sequence-diagrams among them): key infrastructure for the browser-diagramming decade.

## Context

Lucidchart's development also began in 2008 (prototype that December), but its real launch is 2010 — 2008's defining *shipped* milestone is WebSequenceDiagrams. PlantUML, which generalized text-to-diagram across many types, followed in 2009. Creately launched publicly at TechCrunch50 (October 2008), and XMind went open source in November.
