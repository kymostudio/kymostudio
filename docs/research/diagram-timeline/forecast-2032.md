## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Lucid's final listed year

Sixteen consecutive years on the list (2010–2025 measured, 2026–2032 projected) end the way long tenures on this timeline usually do: quietly, with the product still profitable and the selection events happening elsewhere. With Visio (2028) and Lucid gone, no tool whose primary interface is a human dragging shapes remains in the top five.

### 2. The open canvas commons becomes infrastructure

Excalidraw and diagrams.net settle into the role open-source eventually wins in every layer: the neutral, embeddable, self-hostable substrate. They stop competing for mindshare and start being assumed — the SQLite of canvases.

### 3. Context graphs reach the types top 3

Two years after entering, the context graph passes the system-architecture diagram itself. The map of *what we know and who owns it* turns out to matter more, day to day, than the map of *how the services connect* — because agents redraw the latter on demand from the former.

## Context

User journey diagrams exit the types list after 18 listed years (first listed 2013) — the UX-era artifact most tied to humans hand-synthesizing research, which is precisely the work that moved into models. The journey *insight* survives; the hand-drawn journey *map* becomes a generated view.
