## Highlights

### 1. GitHub renders Mermaid natively; D2 open-sourced

In **February 2022** GitHub began natively rendering **Mermaid** in Markdown — READMEs, issues, PRs, wikis. A fenced ` ```mermaid ` block now *is* a diagram, in front of tens of millions of developers: the single most important diagram-as-code event of the decade, pushing Mermaid past 8M users. In **November 2022** Terrastruct open-sourced **D2**, a modern declarative diagram language with first-class theming and an autoformatter — proof the language itself could be rethought. Both analyzed in `RES-MERMAID-D2-001`.

### 2. Miro raises $400M Series C at $17.5B

Closed 5 January 2022, led by ICONIQ Growth; users had grown 500% since 2020 to 30M, with Atlassian and Salesforce Ventures joining. The peak-valuation moment for visual collaboration — the year Miro became the category's biggest company.

### 3. Apple ships Freeform

Released 13 December 2022 (iOS 16.2/macOS 13.1): a free infinite-canvas whiteboard **preinstalled on every iPhone, iPad and Mac**, with FaceTime-integrated collaboration. The clearest signal that freeform diagramming had become a platform-level commodity. (Obsidian Canvas shipped the same month.)

## Context

GitLab, Notion and Obsidian had been adding Mermaid support; GitHub's move settled the question. The text-to-diagram thread that began with DOT (2000) → WebSequenceDiagrams (2008) → PlantUML (2009) → Mermaid (2014) reached escape velocity here — the world kymo launches into.
