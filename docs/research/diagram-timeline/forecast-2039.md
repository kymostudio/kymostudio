## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The diagram of record is generated

Live-architecture platforms take #1 in tools. For the systems organizations care most about, the authoritative diagram — the one in the audit, the one in the incident review, the one new engineers are onboarded with — is generated from the system itself. Hand-drawn architecture survives as proposal and pedagogy; authority has moved.

### 2. The agent org chart becomes a compliance artifact

Regulators and insurers converge on a requirement first sketched in the late-2030s governance regimes: organizations must be able to show, current as of today, which automated systems act on their behalf, with what authority, under whose accountability. The agent org chart at #3 is the rare diagram type whose growth is legally mandated.

### 3. What's left to draw: the sketch endures

Measured at the other end of the spectrum: freehand sketching — on whiteboards, tablets, napkins — persists essentially unchanged, as the thinking tool it always was. The timeline's lesson repeats: media replace each other's *authority*, almost never each other's *thinking function*.
