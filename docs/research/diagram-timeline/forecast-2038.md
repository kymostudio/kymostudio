## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Live-architecture platforms reach #2

One slot below the top, the category's growth question flips from "will teams adopt derived views?" to "what is left for the surfaces where humans drew?" The answer, increasingly: intent, proposals, and teaching — everything *about* the system that is not *in* the system yet.

### 2. The trace diagram is the debugger of the agent age

Incident reviews for agent systems standardize on rendered interaction traces — who delegated what to whom, with which context, and where it went wrong. The sequence-diagram lineage, third wind: 1997 UML artifact, 2030s telemetry view, now the primary forensic surface for systems whose actors are not all human.

### 3. Spatial review reaches #4 in tools

The room-scale design review — humans and remote agents walking a shared spatial scene of the system — becomes the default ceremony for major architecture decisions in large organizations, a decade after the hardware made it possible and three years after it stopped requiring a specialist to set up.

## Context

A deepening year, not a churn year: no list entries or exits, only the era's logic compounding. The human role consolidates around verification — the diagrams most looked at are ones nobody made, checked by people whose job is to disbelieve them.
