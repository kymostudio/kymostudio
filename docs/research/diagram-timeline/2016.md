## Highlights

### 1. Figma launches publicly

On **27 September 2016**, **Figma released publicly** (v1.0.0) — anyone could sign up and use the browser-based collaborative design tool for free. Files stopped being attachments and became URLs; "design review" became opening a link. The desktop-file era of visual work effectively ends here, and expectations reset for every visual tool, diagramming included.

### 2. The Open Group releases ArchiMate 3.0

Released 14 June 2016 — the first major update to the EA modeling language since 2013, adding strategy elements (capability, resource, outcome) and physical-world modeling, aligned with TOGAF, BPMN and UML. It cemented ArchiMate as the enterprise-architecture diagramming standard and drove a tooling wave (Archi 4, BiZZdesign).

### 3. Lucid Software raises $36M Series B

Closed 14 September 2016, led by Spectrum Equity, to push Lucidchart deeper into the enterprise as the web-native Visio alternative — early proof that browser diagramming was a venture-scale business.

## Context

Figma's free tier and zero-install onboarding did to Sketch and Adobe what Lucidchart had done to Visio — faster and more completely. The browser had won as the place visual work happens, and Figma's later expansion into whiteboarding (FigJam, 2021) and AI starts from this base.
