## Highlights

### 1. Lucidchart launches — HTML5 real-time collaborative diagramming

**Lucidchart** launched publicly in July 2010: an early **fully HTML5** — no Flash — diagramming app with **real-time multi-user editing**, integrated into the Google Apps Marketplace. It proved browser diagramming could match desktop tools without a plugin, and that multiplayer editing was both possible and desirable — the template for the collaborative era, previewing what Figma would generalize to all of design.

### 2. Visio 2010 ships validation rules and containers

Available May 2010, the Premium edition added a diagram **Validation API** (rule-checking for BPMN/flowchart correctness) plus structured containers, lists and callouts. The dominant desktop tool moved from free-form drawing toward *semantically checked* process diagrams — chasing the BPM wave.

### 3. Google Drawings launches inside Google Docs

Launched 12 April 2010 as a real-time collaborative drawings editor pitched for "organizational charts, flow charts, design diagrams," insertable into Docs/Sheets/Slides. A free, zero-install diagram canvas bundled with the world's biggest office suite normalized casual cloud diagramming for the mass market.

## Context

Lucid Software was formally founded around October 2010 (development since 2008). Other 2010 markers: OmniGraffle for iPad shipped as an iPad launch app — first serious touch diagramming — and Archi 1.0, the free ArchiMate tool, arrived in June.
