## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The dedicated surface beats the general assistant

Agent-design platforms overtake the assistant suites for #1. The pattern is an old one: the general interface wins the adoption race, then the purpose-built environment wins the daily-work race — IDEs over text editors, Figma over browser mockup tools. Where systems are actually designed, the surface that holds proposals, diffs, ownership, and history beats the chat that started the work.

### 2. Structured intent replaces the prompt

The interaction model matures past conversation: humans express constraints and goals in structured form (often visually), agents return candidate designs as models, and the loop iterates on artifacts rather than sentences. The prompt survives at the edges; the center of gravity is intent-in, diagram-out.

### 3. Lucid begins its exit

The strongest SaaS-era independent enters its final two listed years. Its 100M-user base does not vanish — it ages, the way Visio's did, while new selection events happen elsewhere. The visual-collaboration generation that defined 2010–2022 is now the incumbent generation.
