## Highlights

### 1. Figma's public API — the canvas becomes programmable

In **March 2018** Figma shipped its **public API** — programmatic read access to files, nodes, and rendered images (the plugin platform followed in 2019). A canvas stopped being just an app and became a **platform**: diagrams and designs as *data* external tools can read, render and mutate. Every 2023–2025 "AI edits the canvas" demo runs through interfaces this turn opened.

### 2. RealtimeBoard (later Miro) raises $25M Series A

Closed 8 November 2018, led by Accel; the whiteboarding platform had 2M users and 8,000 paying teams (Netflix, Twitter, Salesforce). The round funded the scale-up that preceded the 2019 Miro rebrand and made freeform visual collaboration a category.

### 3. C4-PlantUML created

Ricardo Niepel's library (September 2018) combined Simon Brown's C4 model with PlantUML macros, making C4 context/container/component diagrams writable as plain text in any repo — the bridge that took C4 from conference talks into everyday engineering docs. PlantUML's official standard library (bundling AWS/Azure/C4 icon sets) also dates to 2018.

## Context
