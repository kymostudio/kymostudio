## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Visio exits the top 10 — the first time in the timeline's 34 years

The tool that held #1 for 27 straight years (1995–2021) drops off the list entirely. Nothing dramatic happens; that is the point. The Microsoft 365 bundle keeps a legacy floor of usage, but a floor is not a ranking, and the organizations that once standardized on Visio now standardize on whatever their assistant emits.

### 2. Declarative DSLs find their durable niche

D2-class languages enter the list: not as Mermaid competitors for casual diagrams, but as the infrastructure-as-code teams' choice where diagrams are versioned, reviewed, and owned like Terraform. The lesson of the 2020s holds — text formats survive by attaching to an engineering workflow, not by competing on syntax.

### 3. Suite gravity concentrates the human canvas

For the diagrams humans still draw by hand, the gravity is the design suite: FigJam-class surfaces bundled with the tools where product work already lives. Standalone whiteboards keep losing the default-choice battle they started losing when Figma went multiplayer in 2016.

## Context

A symbolic year: the desktop era's last anchor leaves, twenty-eight years after Microsoft bought it. The timeline's old joke — "Visio is the floor" — passes to diagrams.net, which will hold the self-hosted floor for decades.
