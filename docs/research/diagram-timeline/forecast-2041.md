## Highlights (projected)

### 1. After 46 consecutive years, flowchart yields #1

The longest run in this timeline ends: the live system view becomes the most-encountered diagram type. The torch passes from the form that *explains a process someone designed* to the form that *shows a system as it currently is*. Flowchart settles at #2, where this forecast keeps it for years — the form is immortal; the rank was not.

### 2. Mermaid exits the tools list

Twenty-seven years after creation and after a 2026 stint at #1, Mermaid leaves the list the way Graphviz did before it: not dead but dissolved — its layout ideas, its as-code discipline, and its agent-default role all absorbed into the toolchains it made possible. On this timeline, infrastructure exits are graduations.

### 3. What "a diagram" means has quietly changed

A diagram in 2041 is, definitionally: a query against a model, plus a rendering contract, plus a provenance trail. The 1995 definition — marks a person arranged on a surface — survives as one (cherished) special case. The vocabulary follows: people say "view" where they used to say "diagram," the way "site" replaced "homepage."

## Context

The era's hinge year. Both handovers land at once, and both beneficiaries are things no one draws: the live view at #1 in types, and (next year) the provenance layer entering tools — because once nobody draws the authoritative diagram, *trusting* it becomes the scarce thing.
