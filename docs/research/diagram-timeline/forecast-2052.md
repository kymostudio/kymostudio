## Highlights (projected)

### 1. diagrams.net at #5 — the Visio role, two eras on

Forty-one years after launching as Diagramly, the self-hosted open canvas climbs back to #5: the place organizations that trust nothing ambient still draw. Every era has needed a floor — desktop Visio (1995–2010s), then diagrams.net for the security-first (2020s), now diagrams.net again for the provenance-skeptical. The floor's name stopped changing; only the anxiety above it changes.

### 2. The intent map reaches #2 in types

Only the live view ranks above it. A normative human artifact at #2, surrounded by derived machine views, is Era F's thesis in a single table row: what humans uniquely contribute became *the second most important picture in the world*.

### 3. Sixty-year-old SVGs still render

An archival milestone with infrastructure implications: SVG 1.0-era files from 2001 open pixel-perfect in 2052 renderers, outliving four generations of proprietary formats. The W3C-substrate bet this timeline traced from PGML/VML (1998) pays its longest dividend — and the era's archivists notice that *derived* views, by contrast, are only as immortal as their sources.
