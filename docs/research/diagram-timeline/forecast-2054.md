## Highlights (projected)

### 1. A stable top four: summon, verify, derive, inhabit

Ambient assistants, provenance layers, live-architecture platforms, spatial canvases — the four verbs of the mature stack, in rank order, unchanged in their membership for a decade. Each is the survivor of a category fight this timeline watched: assistants (2023–), trust layers (2042–), live views (2036–), spatial (2033–).

### 2. The 1990s notations refuse to die

At the foot of the types list, the state-machine and sequence lineages swap places for the *n*th time — both on this list, under shifting names, since the UML era. Sixty years of paradigm turnover never produced a better picture of "what states can this thing be in" or "who said what to whom in what order." Some problems have a final form.

### 3. Excalidraw's 34th year — the open canvas as a public good

Still at #5, still open-source, still where humans go when the thinking is messy. Its hand-drawn aesthetic — a 2020 design choice — turned out to be the most durable UI decision on the tools list: the visual signal for "this is a thought, not an assertion" became more valuable, not less, in a world of authoritative derived views.
