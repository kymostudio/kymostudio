## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Intent & policy maps enter the types list

The decade's defining genre arrives: visual artifacts answering *what is this system allowed to want, and within which boundaries may it act?* Goal hierarchies, authority boundaries, escalation thresholds, forbidden regions — drawn, because the people who must ratify them are not the people who can read the formal policy underneath. It enters at #5, the highest debut in the forecast period.

### 2. The explanation duty becomes visual

The governance regimes that matured through the 2030s converge on a practical requirement: systems exercising delegated authority must maintain a *current, human-legible* representation of that authority. Legible, in practice, means diagrammatic — and the intent map becomes to the 2050s what the privacy policy was to the 2010s, with better tooling and (the forecast hopes) more readers.

### 3. Provenance holds the tools top 4

A non-event worth recording: trust infrastructure neither grows nor shrinks — it has reached the steady share that verification layers occupy in every mature stack. The era's furniture is in place.

## Context

Every era of this timeline gave diagrams a new subject: structure (A), process (B), collaboration (C), agency (D), reality (E). Era F's subject is *intention* — the one thing in the stack that still originates exclusively with humans, and therefore the one thing that most needs a shared picture.
