## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The intent map climbs to #4: diagrams as agreement artifacts

One year after entering, the intent map passes the agent org chart. The growth driver is the same one that powered every fast climb in the forecast: attachment to decisions. Intent maps are *ratified*, versioned, and cited in disputes — the genre inherits the social weight that org charts, contracts, and constitutions carry, with a rendering pipeline.

### 2. Diagram literacy inverts in education

Curricula catch up with the era: students are taught to *read* derived views critically — provenance first, freshness second, derivation chain third — before they are taught to draw anything. Drawing-first diagram education, standard since flowchart templates entered classrooms in the 1970s, becomes the specialist track. The mass skill is interrogating pictures machines made.

### 3. The ER diagram is still here — data never dies

Noted at #9: the entity-relationship diagram, on this list for all 55 of its years, two slots above the sequence lineage. Schemas outlive paradigms because data outlives programs; Chen's 1976 notation has now coexisted with six eras of this timeline and shows no sign of leaving before the dataset does.

## Context

A cultural-consolidation year. The artifacts stopped changing; the people changed around them. When the median professional's diagram skill is critical reading rather than authoring, Era F's "shared cognitive medium" label stops being a metaphor.
