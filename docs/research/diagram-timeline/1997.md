## Highlights

### 1. UML 1.1 adopted by the OMG

The UML Partners submitted a UML 1.0 draft to the OMG in January 1997; the refined **UML 1.1** followed in August and was **formally adopted in November 1997**. OMG adoption — the single most important software-modeling milestone of the decade — converted UML from a vendor proposal into a ratified standard any tool could implement. For the next two decades, "draw the design" effectively meant "draw it in UML."

### 2. Visio 5.0 product line ships

Visio unveiled the **5.0 line** in fall 1997 (Standard $149 / Professional $349 / Technical $349) on a common 32-bit engine with diagram "wizards" — the version that solidified Visio's three-tier strategy and its IT/network-diagram push, right as corporate networks exploded.

### 3. Fowler's *UML Distilled* — the first book on UML

Martin Fowler's *UML Distilled* (Addison-Wesley, 1997) was the first UML book to market, won the year's Software Development Magazine Productivity Award, and eventually sold 250k+ copies. It made UML diagrams learnable in an afternoon and did as much as the spec itself to spread the notation through working developers.

## Context

The exact adoption day is not recorded in OMG summaries; authoritative sources cite **November 1997**. UML 1.x then iterated (1.3, 1.4, 1.5) before the larger UML 2.0 overhaul. Every modeling tool of the era — Rose, Together, and later Enterprise Architect — orbits this decision.
