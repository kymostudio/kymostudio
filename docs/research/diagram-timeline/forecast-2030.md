## Highlights (projected)

### 1. "AI-generated diagrams" retires from the types list

The category that entered in 2023 leaves after seven years — not in decline but in victory. When the large majority of diagrams are machine-generated, generation is a property of every type rather than a type of its own, and the list stops counting it. The same thing happened to "web-based" as a tool qualifier circa 2015.

### 2. Context graphs enter: teams maintain maps to brief their machines

Organizations discover that the limiting factor on agent usefulness is shared context, and the artifact that carries it is a curated graph — systems, owners, constraints, decisions — kept current the way wikis never were, because agents both consume and groom it. The knowledge-map lineage (concept maps, 2004) returns with a consumer that actually reads it.

### 3. Agent-design platforms reach #2

One year after entering the list, the category sits behind only the assistants themselves. The growth is mechanical: every organization that gives agents real responsibility needs a surface where humans can see — and veto — what those agents intend to build.

## Context

The mid-era settling: assistants in front, agent-design platforms beneath them, interchange formats underneath everything. The types list begins reorganizing around what agents produce (workflow graphs, context graphs) rather than what humans drew (journeys, mind maps — both now in their final listed years).

## Signals

- Concept-mapping institutionalization (CMC 2004, CmapTools) — the lineage context graphs revive
- "Context is the bottleneck" — the 2025 agent-engineering consensus (context windows, retrieval, memory systems)
