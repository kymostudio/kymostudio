## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The Era E stack settles

Live views at #1, assistants at #2, spatial and provenance behind them: the decade ends with its categories mature and its churn over. An architecture decision in 2045 touches every layer the era built — a derived view, a simulated alternative, a signed attestation, a spatial review — and none of them existed as products in 2030.

### 2. Simulation views reach #4 in types

Two years from entry to the top 5 — the fastest climb of the forecast period — because simulation attached itself to decisions rather than documents. Diagram genres that gate actions grow faster than genres that describe states; the whole forecast period obeys this rule.

### 3. The human role finishes its migration

The era's closing inventory of what humans uniquely do with diagrams: choose which views exist, set the thresholds that make a simulation a verdict, disbelieve the right derivation at the right moment, and explain systems to other humans. Everything else — drawing, updating, laying out, even noticing — has a machine doing the routine case.

## Context

Between 2036 and 2045 the question moved from "is this diagram up to date?" to "do we trust what derives our diagrams — and do we mean the same thing by them?" That second clause is Era F's opening question.
