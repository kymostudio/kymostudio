## Highlights

### 1. Gliffy founded — diagramming moves into the browser

**Gliffy** was founded in 2005 in San Francisco by Chris Kohlhardt and Clint Dickson — one of the first credible **browser-based** alternatives to desktop Visio. It marks the start of the Web & SaaS era: the diagram leaves the desktop file and lives at a URL. Its early Atlassian connection led to Confluence/Jira integrations that embedded diagramming into developer workflows — a pattern every later web tool copied.

### 2. UML 2.0 formally published by the OMG

After years of "adopted" drafts, the OMG issued the *formal* **UML 2.0** Superstructure/Infrastructure specifications in July 2005 — the biggest revision in UML's history, adding composite-structure, timing, and interaction-overview diagrams on a new metamodel. It defined what every UML tool of the next decade had to implement, at the peak of the model-driven wave.

### 3. BPMI.org merges into the OMG

Announced 29 June 2005: the author of BPMN 1.0 merged its BPM activities into the OMG, putting process notation on the same standards track as UML. This governance event made BPMN an OMG standard the following year and kicked off the 2006–2012 BPM diagramming wave.

## Context

Built initially on the Flash/Flex stack like much of the 2005 web, Gliffy proved demand for no-install diagramming years before HTML5 made it smooth. Lucidchart (2010) and draw.io (2011) would take the idea to its HTML5 conclusion.
