## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Miro exits — the human-canvas franchise completes its arc

The tool that took #1 from Visio in 2022, rode the remote-work explosion to 90M users, and defined the multiplayer-whiteboard category leaves the list after 18 consecutive years (listed since 2018, founded 2011 as RealtimeBoard). Like Visio before it, it exits as a healthy business whose category stopped being where the new decisions happen.

### 2. The agent-native stack is settled

Era D ends with a stable stack: assistants in front, agent-design platforms as the workbench, canvas SDKs as the substrate, structured models as the interchange, deterministic renderers underneath. Ten years earlier every layer of this was a startup pitch; now it is the boring default, which is what winning looks like on this timeline.

### 3. The types list completes its agent turn

Of the 2025 types list, only the durable cores remain — flowchart, ER, architecture, the sequence lineage — surrounded by artifacts that did not exist as categories in 2025: workflow graphs at #2, context graphs at #3, behavior maps at #4. The mind map, on the list since 2000, enters its final listed year.
