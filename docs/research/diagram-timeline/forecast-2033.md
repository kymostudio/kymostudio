## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Spatial canvases enter the list — a decade after the headsets

AR/spatial diagram review reaches ordinary teams: an architecture walk-through as a shared spatial scene, anchored to the same structured model the flat SVG renders. It took ten years of hardware iteration after 2023's headset wave for this to stop being a demo — and what finally lands is *viewing*, not authoring; the diagrams are still born flat.

### 2. Model-behavior maps enter: safety reviews need a picture

As model-governance regimes mature, evaluation and safety reviews standardize on visual artifacts — maps of capability surfaces, failure modes, and mitigations. The model-behavior map enters the types list as the first diagram genre whose primary audience is a regulator.

### 3. The trace diagram revives the sequence-diagram lineage

UML sequence diagrams — on this list since 1997 under one name or another — get their second wind as **interaction/trace diagrams**: rendered views of what a fleet of agents actually did, read during incident review. Telemetry did for the sequence diagram what BPM did for the flowchart: gave it a data source.

## Context

Era D's back half begins: the artifacts are increasingly *read from systems* rather than *written by people* — traces, behavior maps, context graphs. That current carries straight into Era E.
