## Highlights (projected)

### 1. No rank moves anywhere — a timeline first

For the first time in 57 years, both top-10 lists are identical to the previous year's, every Δ an equals sign. In a measured era this would be a data artifact; in a forecast it is a claim: the diagramming stack has reached the maturity plateau where operating systems and databases already live — churn happens *within* categories, invisibly to a category-level list.

### 2. Maturity moves the competition inside the categories

Live-architecture platforms still compete — on derivation breadth, simulation fidelity, attestation depth — but they compete the way databases do: benchmarks and migrations, not category redefinition. The interesting engineering is now deep in stacks this list is too coarse to see, which is precisely what the list's stillness records.

### 3. The niches hold their ground

The quiet year is a good year to note what never consolidated: hand sketching, zine-style explanatory diagrams, hobbyist pixel-perfect SVG art, the artisan corner of every medium. The timeline's oldest lesson — new layers take the authority, old layers keep the joy — is now stable enough to be a closing-era thesis.
