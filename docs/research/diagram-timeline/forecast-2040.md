## Highlights (projected)

### 1. The 46th and final year of the flowchart's reign

Flowchart has been the #1 diagram type every single year of this timeline — through UML's rise and fall, the SaaS wave, the AI turn. This is the last year it holds. The form is not declining; everything else is being overtaken by views derived from live systems, and the live view is about to overtake the one type that was always drawn by hand to explain *intent* rather than record *state*.

### 2. Mermaid's text syntax fades from view

In its 26th year of existence and 22nd on this list, Mermaid enters its final stretch ranked, not because the format died but because nobody sees it anymore: agents exchange structured models directly, and the human-readable syntax that made 2014–2030 diagram-as-code legible becomes an internal representation, like SQL inside an ORM.

### 3. Diagrams on demand, on any surface

The display layer finishes commoditizing: any view, rendered to any surface — IDE pane, document margin, wall display, spatial room — from the same model, with layout solved per-medium by the rendering layer. "Where do diagrams live?" — the question that defined all three measured eras — stops having an answer more specific than "wherever you look."
