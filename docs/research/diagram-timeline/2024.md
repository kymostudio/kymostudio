## Highlights

### 1. Mermaid Chart raises $7.5M; Figma AI

On **20 March 2024** **Mermaid Chart** announced a $7.5M seed round (Open Core Ventures, Sequoia, Microsoft's M12), shipping an AI chat that generates and repairs diagrams plus a visual editor over Mermaid source. Serious investors backing a *diagram-as-code* company confirmed the text-based diagram language as a venture-scale asset. At Config 2024 (June), **Figma unveiled Figma AI** (Make Designs, Visual Search). The mature product pattern: AI generates source, a visual editor edits the same source, text stays the single source of truth.

### 2. tldraw ships SDK 2.0

Released 14 March 2024: the whiteboard library's first stable commercial SDK release, positioning tldraw as the "build your own infinite canvas" infrastructure layer for other products — the canvas-as-component business model its 2025 Series A then funded.

### 3. Claude Artifacts launches and renders Mermaid natively

Launched June 2024 with Claude 3.5 Sonnet (GA August): Anthropic's Artifacts panel renders Mermaid blocks, SVG, and React live in chat — making "describe a diagram, watch it render" a default LLM interaction and entrenching Mermaid as the lingua franca of AI-generated diagrams. (ChatGPT canvas, October 2024, is the same trend.)

## Context

Note: a "D2 1.0" sometimes cited for 2024 does not exist — D2 remained pre-1.0 (v0.6–0.7). The durable 2024 motif is **bidirectional editing over text source** (chat ⇄ code ⇄ canvas), which every serious tool now pursues — and which is exactly kymo's editor architecture.
