## Highlights

### 1. OmniGraffle 2.0 and the native-macOS diagramming wave

**OmniGraffle 2.0** (April 2002) added layered canvases and advanced shape tools, cementing OmniGraffle as the flagship native macOS diagramming app. It defined a *taste* for diagramming — clean defaults, layout assist, beautiful typography — that distinguished it from Visio's utilitarian feel; that design-forward sensibility is a direct ancestor of the polish later web tools (Lucidchart, Figma, Whimsical) competed on.

### 2. IBM agrees to buy Rational Software for ~$2.1B

Announced 6 December 2002: the decade's biggest modeling-tools deal. The UML market leader — home of the Three Amigos, Rose, and RUP — became IBM's fifth software brand, signalling that visual modeling was now big-vendor platform territory rather than an independent tools market (the deal closed February 2003).

### 3. Borland acquires TogetherSoft for ~$185M

Announced 29–30 October 2002. TogetherSoft's ControlCenter, famed for "LiveSource" simultaneous round-trip between UML diagrams and code, was Rose's sharpest challenger; Borland's purchase was the other half of the UML-tool consolidation wave triggered that autumn.

## Context

2002 has no dominant standards milestone — the year's real story is consolidation: within weeks, both leading independent UML vendors agreed to disappear into platform companies. UML 2.0 submissions were in progress (adopted 2003, published 2005), and Sparx Enterprise Architect was gaining traction at the low end.
