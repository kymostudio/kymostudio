## Highlights (projected)

### 1. Live-architecture platforms enter: nobody drew this diagram

A new tool category enters the list whose product is a diagram no one drew: views derived continuously from the running system — deployment topology, data flows, ownership — rendered on demand from telemetry, configuration, and the service catalog. The software catalog generation (Backstage, 2020) supplied the data; Era D's structured-model rendering supplied the output; Era E connects them.

### 2. The live system view enters the types list at #3

The artifact itself — an architecture diagram that is *a query result with a rendering contract* — debuts as the fastest-rising type of the decade. Its defining property: it cannot be out of date, because it is not a record of anyone's belief about the system. It is the system, projected.

### 3. C4 exits, absorbed rather than defeated

C4's exit after three decades of the structured-architecture idea (on this list since 2012-era ArchiMate/C4 consolidation) is the era transition in miniature: its layered zoom — context, container, component — survives as the navigation metaphor of every live view, while the hand-maintained C4 *document* disappears.

## Context

Era E's defining shift: *where Era D made diagrams something agents could write, Era E makes the important ones something nobody writes.* Human work moves up a level — deciding which views exist, verifying them, and acting on what they show.
